# ⚰️ POLYCRISIS TRAIL

A satirical Oregon Trail clone where you're just trying to survive 2026. The polycrisis is the antagonist — everything is on fire simultaneously, and that's the joke.

## Premise

Cross the hellscape of modern existence from January to December. Every month is a landmark on the trail. Everything wants to kill you. Now featuring all crises active simultaneously: the climate you ignored, the aliens nobody will confirm, the cosmic horrors rising from the deep, and more. Everything on fire at once. That's the joke.

## Crisis Themes

Seven doom layers, all active simultaneously. Each follows the same structure: dedicated months, unique mechanics, own random event pool, own death causes, own mini-game.

### 🌍 CLIMATE — The Slow Burn

*You knew it was coming. You did nothing. Now breathe the smoke.*

- **Months:** April (tornados), May (wildfires), June (heat dome), August (hurricanes), November (supply chain)
- **Mechanic:** Weather events deal direct health damage, block travel, spike supply costs. Physical consequences, no ambiguity about what's happening.
- **Damage type:** Health, Supplies, Mobility

### 👽 ALIENS / DISCLOSURE — The Ambiguity Weapon

*The government confirms nothing. That's somehow worse than confirmation.*

- **Months:** March (leaked footage), July (congressional hearing), October (October Surprise)
- **Mechanic:** Ambiguity IS the damage type. UAP events drain Hope and Morale via uncertainty, not health. Party members fracture along belief/disbelief lines. Perception cascade does more damage than any physical event.
- **Damage type:** Hope, Morale, Party Cohesion
- **Special:** Alien events can trigger "ontological shock" debuff — affected party member is temporarily useless while reconsidering their place in the cosmos.

### 🐙 CTHULHU / COSMIC HORROR — The Certain-of-Incomprehensible

*The Old Ones are rising. The planning commission hasn't approved the zoning yet.*

- **Months:** February (strange dreams), April (Innsmouth anomalies), June (coastal incidents), September (cult proliferation), December (stars align)
- **Mechanic:** Sanity — a new stat. Unlike Hope (which drains from despair), Sanity drains from exposure to truths humans weren't meant to know. Low Sanity causes wrong choices to look like correct ones. Unlike Climate (predictable) or Aliens (ambiguous), Cthulhu events are certain — you just can't comprehend them. The horror is knowing something is happening and being unable to describe it to anyone.
- **Damage type:** Sanity, Hope
- **Special:** Sanity below 20 inverts some UI elements. Text gets… wrong. Options reorder. The game gaslights you.

### 🤖 AI SINGULARITY — The Autocomplete Apocalypse

*The AGI emerges. It immediately asks for a subscription.*

- **Months:** February (first AGI rumors), May (mass layoffs begin), September (AI regulation panic), November (AGI announcement)
- **Mechanic:** Agency loss. AI doesn't damage you directly — it replaces your choices. Some events auto-resolve without your input. The game occasionally plays itself. Decisions that used to be yours arrive pre-filled. The autocomplete is efficient. The autocomplete is not on your side.
- **Damage type:** Agency, Money, Identity
- **Special:** "Automation" debuff — party member gets replaced by an AI version. The AI is more efficient (+stats) but has zero loyalty and makes inhumanly logical decisions ("let the Gig Worker die, they're a net drain on party resources"). Some events show two sets of options: yours and the AI's. You may not realize which one you picked.

### 🦠 COVID 2 — The Sequel Nobody Greenlit

*WHO declares emergency. Everyone collectively shrugs.*

- **Months:** January (novel virus detected in Wuhan), March (WHO declares pandemic), June (summer surge), October (winter wave begins)
- **Mechanic:** Contagion. Party members infect each other. The disease is novel but the discourse is identical. Masks come back. The same people are mad about it. Protocol fatigue means nobody listens anymore. The real damage is arguing about it.
- **Damage type:** Health, Morale, Party Cohesion
- **Special:** "Long COVID" debuff — random stat penalties that never fully resolve. Some party members get it, some don't. It's never fully clear who has it and who's just exhausted from everything else. Healthcare Worker can treat but burns out faster with each wave.

### 🏢 KAIJU — The Property Value Apocalypse

*Godzilla is real. He's tied up in environmental impact litigation.*

- **Months:** March (Pacific sonar anomaly), June (first emergence — Tokyo), August (second emergence), November (third emergence)
- **Mechanic:** Infrastructure Collapse. Kaiju don't target you — they destroy the world around you. Supply routes severed, cities evacuated, power grids fail, insurance calls it "wind damage." You're not the protagonist. You're just in the blast radius.
- **Damage type:** Supplies, Mobility, Money
- **Special:** "Evacuation" mechanic — choose: flee (lose all progress for the month), shelter in place (health risk), or bet on the Jaeger deployment (30% success, 70% the Jaeger falls on a hospital). Jaegers are contractor-built and over budget. The pilots are gig workers.

### 💰 NEO-FEUDALISM — The Quiet Apocalypse

*The rich get richer. You get a subscription for oxygen.*

- **Months:** April (rent increase), July (healthcare denial), September (debt collection), December (wage theft)
- **Mechanic:** Class mobility — downward only. A hidden "Class" stat determines which choices you can even see. Slip below the threshold? Options vanish — you can't afford to see them. The rich get events with options. The poor get events without them. The game has a 1% chance of giving you a "trust fund" event that trivializes half the mechanics, just to make everyone else feel worse.
- **Damage type:** Money, Hope, Options
- **Special:** "Debt Trap" — Money can go negative. Negative money compounds. Some solutions require Class > threshold to access. Some characters start rich. Most don't. The gap widens passively. You don't notice until you can't afford to notice.

---

All seven crises are active in every run. This isn't a menu — it's the polycrisis. Everything hits at once, events overlap, and the horror is the simultaneity. You'll lose health to COVID while your job is automated while a kaiju destroys the supply route while your landlord raises rent while a congressional hearing about aliens offers no useful information while the Old Ones stir in their dreaming — sometimes in the same month, sometimes in the same event.

Cross-theme events (see below) are where the game earns its name: cascading crises where a kaiju attack exposes a buried elder sign that triggers a sanity check while you can't afford the medical treatment because your insurance was AI-denied.

## The Party

Pick 4 party members. Each has a job that gives minor bonuses and theme-specific traits:

- **Gig Worker** — hustles extra supplies, fragile morale. Climate: resistant to supply chain events. AI: job auto-replaced mid-run, adapts fastest. Neo-Feudalism: already poor, immune to downward class shocks.
- **Tech Bro** — starts with more money, randomly loses skills to AI. AI: can "align" with AI (risky buff). Kaiju: "we can disrupt the kaiju space." Fails.
- **Teacher** — high morale boost for others, paid in peanuts. COVID: masks up first, teaches remote (party gets sanity buff). Neo-Feudalism: chronically underpaid, class slowly erodes.
- **Boomer** — resistant to misinformation, can't use technology. AI: completely immune ("I don't use apps"). COVID: "this is just like the flu" (options hidden from other party members).
- **Gen Z** — high stamina, low budget, communicates exclusively in slang. AI: already uses AI for everything, gains productivity. Neo-Feudalism: "we're all gonna die broke anyway." Morale barely moves.
- **Healthcare Worker** — heals party members, burned out. COVID: essential, never gets hazard pay, burnout accelerates. Cthulhu: sanity drains faster (has seen worse in the ER).
- **Influencer** — can "manifest" supplies (unreliable), high drama. Aliens: claims contact, gets book deal. Kaiju: livestreams the attack, dies.
- **Conspiracy Theorist** — immune to alien ambiguity, fragile sanity. AI: "the algorithm is GIVING you these choices for a reason." Sometimes right.
- **Cultist** — high sanity resistance, periodically drains party morale to "share the truth." Cthulhu events buff this character.
- **AI Researcher** — can predict AI events, fragile morale ("I created this?"). AI: sees hidden AI choices before they fire. Tragic arc.
- **Essential Worker** — resistant to plague, gets no hazard pay, high burnout. COVID: immune to infection, morale drains from being called a "hero" while denied a raise.
- **Disaster Prepper** — resistant to kaiju/climate events, spends all money on bunker supplies, insufferable about it. Kaiju: "told you so" buff (other party members lose morale).
- **Debt Slave** — starts with negative money, high stamina, Class locked at bottom. Neo-Feudalism: debt compounds. Can never escape. Best stamina in the game. Nobody wants to play as them.
- **Venture Capitalist** — starts with enormous money, zero empathy. Neo-Feudalism: Class locked at top. Events show them profiting from disasters affecting everyone else. Morale never drops. Nobody wants to play WITH them.

## Core Stats

- **Health** (0-100, everyone has it)
- **Morale** (party-wide, affects event outcomes)
- **Money** (shared, evaporates fast; can go negative — compounds as debt)
- **Supplies** (food, medicine, fuel, phone battery)
- **Hope** (secret stat — when it hits 0, game over even if alive)
- **Sanity** (party-wide, Cthulhu theme — starts at 100, effects invert below 20)
- **Agency** (party-wide, AI theme — starts at 100. Below 50, the game starts auto-selecting choices. Below 20, you're watching, not playing.)
- **Class** (per-member, Neo-Feudalism — determines which choices you can see, 1-10. Below 3, menu options are hidden. Below 1, you get one option per event.)
- **Infection** (per-member, COVID theme — 0-100. Contagiousness increases with value. Healthcare Worker reduces others' Infection.)

## Mini-Games

Theme-specific mini-games appear during months when that theme is most active. Multiple mini-games can fire in the same month.

### Base Mini-Games

- **Hunting** → "Gig Hunting" — scroll through fake job postings, dodge scams
- **River Crossing** → "Fording Bureaucracy" — renew a license at the DMV
- **Fishing** → "Side Hustle Fishing" — catch freelance gigs, some are scams
- **Trading** → "Marketplace" — trade with other survivors, get scammed sometimes

### 👽 Alien — "Congressional Briefing"

Extract actionable information from a UAP hearing. Multiple choice, every answer is technically true and completely useless:
- **Ask a follow-up question** → briefing ends, escorted out. Gain nothing.
- **Accept the answer** → lose Hope. Continue playing.
- **Leak the transcript** → gain Morale, risk "national security" debuff (lose random supplies).
- **Start a podcast about it** → Influencer gains Money, everyone else loses Hope.
- **Demand crash retrieval materials** → Conspiracy Theorist buffed. Everyone else: ontological shock check.

### 🐙 Cthulhu — "The Ritual"

Perform an eldritch ritual from a poorly translated text:
- **Wrong incantation** → lose Sanity, nothing happens
- **Right incantation** → lose Sanity, gain supplies from… somewhere
- **Refuse to participate** → lose Morale (Cultist angry), but Sanity preserved
- **Film it for content** → Influencer gains followers. Sanity loss doubles.
- **Translate it yourself** → Conspiracy Theorist option. Small Sanity loss, large insight gain. Unlocks a secret.

### 🌍 Climate — "Disaster Prep"

Stockpile supplies before the next disaster:
- **Buy in bulk** → spend Money, gain Supplies. Efficient.
- **Panic buy** → spend more Money, neighbors hate you (-Morale), same Supplies.
- **Go feral** → Gig Worker option. Forage successfully. Random: could find supplies or poison ivy.
- **Rely on FEMA** → lose 3 turns waiting. Nothing arrives.

### 🤖 AI — "The Performance Review"

*Appears when Agency < 60.*

An AI evaluates your party's performance. It has perfect data and no understanding of context:
- **Accept the review** → Party member with lowest stats is flagged for "optimization."
- **Dispute the review** → Lose Agency. The appeal is also AI-evaluated.
- **Game the metrics** → Tech Bro option. Risk: the AI notices. Consequence: worse.
- **Unionize** → Gig Worker + Teacher option. Risk: "disruption" debuff. Reward: collective bargaining buff.
- **Bribe the training data** → Conpiracy Theorist option. Works. For now.

### 🦠 COVID — "Contact Tracing"

*Appears when Infection > 30 in party.*

Trace who infected whom. It's always the person who insisted on indoor dining:
- **Full lockdown** → lose all progress this month. No new infections.
- **Selective quarantine** → isolate highest Infection. Others at risk. Morale hit.
- **Ignore it** → Healthcare Worker loses Hope. Infection spreads randomly.
- **Blame China** → Boomer option. Solves nothing. Morale slightly up for Boomer.

### 🏢 Kaiju — "Evacuation Route"

*Appears when kaiju is within strike range.*

Choose your path. The kaiju is 30 minutes out:
- **Official route** → traffic jam. Lose 2 days. Supplies drain.
- **Disaster Prepper's secret route** → faster, but requires trusting the Prepper.
- **Shelter in place** → health risk. If kaiju misses: major flex. If hit: death.
- **Watch the Jaeger fight** → 30% Jaeger wins. 70% Jaeger falls on the shelter.

### 💰 Neo-Feudalism — "The Means Test"

*Appears when any party member drops below Class 3.*

Prove you deserve to survive. Submit documentation:
- **Submit pay stubs** → application denied (uses wrong format).
- **Submit tax returns** → application denied (too much money last year, currently broke).
- **Call your landlord** → automated system. 45 minutes. Disconnected.
- **Just pay the bribe** → Debt Slave can't afford it. Venture Capitalist doesn't understand the question.

## Endings

Multiple endings based on survivors, Hope, Sanity, Agency, and Class:

- **"You Made It"** — at least one survivor, Hope > 0. It's 2027. The bar is on the floor and you cleared it.
- **"The Good Timeline"** — 3+ survivors, Hope > 50, Sanity > 50, Agency > 50. You're not okay but you're okay-er than most.
- **"Climate Victory"** — climate events were the biggest drain. Planet's still dying but YOU lived. Lucky? Yes. Hero? No.
- **"Disclosure"** — alien events dominated. You know the truth. So does everyone else. Nobody cares.
- **"The Dreamer"** — Cthulhu events dominated. You're not sure you survived. The stars are still singing. You listen.
- **"Automated"** — AI events dominated. You survived. The AGI wrote this ending for you. It's more efficient than what you would have written.
- **"Herd Immunity"** — COVID events dominated. Most of your party is dead or disabled. On the bright side, the discourse ended.
- **"Collateral Damage"** — Kaiju events dominated. You lived. Your city didn't. Rebuilding begins. Same contractors.
- **"Barely Surviving"** — Neo-Feudalism events dominated. You made it to 2027 with negative money, Class 1, and no healthcare. Congratulations on your survival. Your bill is in the mail.
- **"Total Party Wipe"** — everyone dead. Standard Oregon Trail experience.
- **"Ascension"** — Sanity hit 0, but Hope survived. You didn't die. You became something else. The save file is corrupted.
- **"Perfect Run"** — all four survivors, all stats above 80. Hidden achievement. Statistically impossible. Somebody cheated.

## Tombstone

Classic Oregon Trail tombstone. Players can write an epitaph. Future runs can see them. Tombstones persist across runs and may be from dead party members from previous playthroughs.

When Sanity < 20: some tombstones appear in languages that don't exist. Some appear before the character dies.

When AI events are high: some tombstones have AI-generated epitaphs. They're grammatically perfect and emotionally hollow.

## Visual Style

- Pixel art aesthetic or simple CSS/canvas
- Retro green/amber terminal colors mixed with modern UI
- Text-heavy, like the original — the writing IS the game
- **Cthulhu:** When Sanity < 20, UI elements degrade. Text shifts subtly. Menu options reorder. The game itself becomes unreliable.
- **Aliens:** Brief "redacted" flickers. Black bar censorship on random text elements during disclosure months.
- **AI:** When Agency < 30, options autocomplete themselves. The cursor moves without your input. You're never sure if you chose or were chosen for.
- **COVID:** Infection icon pulses in the corner. Below 30% Health, the screen gets a fever-haze filter.
- **Kaiju:** Screen shake during active events. Deep bass rumble audio cue. Buildings occasionally fall over in the background art.
- **Neo-Feudalism:** Class indicator in corner. Below Class 3, menu options are literally grayed out. Below Class 1, most of the screen is behind a paywall banner.
