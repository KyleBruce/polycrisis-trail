# ⚰️ POLYCRISIS TRAIL

A satirical Oregon Trail clone where you're just trying to survive 2026. The polycrisis is the antagonist — everything is on fire simultaneously, and that's the joke.

## Premise

Cross the hellscape of modern existence from January to December. Every month is a landmark on the trail. Everything wants to kill you. Now featuring three overlapping apocalypses: the climate you ignored, the aliens nobody will confirm, and the cosmic horrors rising from the deep.

Every run weights the crisis themes differently. No two apocalypses are the same.

## Crisis Themes

Three doom layers. Each follows the same structure: dedicated months, unique mechanics, own random event pool, own death causes, own mini-game. Climate is the slow burn. Aliens are the ambiguity weapon. Cthulhu is the certain-but-incomprehensible.

### 🌍 CLIMATE — The Slow Burn

*You knew it was coming. You did nothing. Now breathe the smoke.*

- **Months:** April (tornados), May (wildfires), June (heat dome), August (hurricanes), November (supply chain)
- **Mechanic:** Weather events deal direct health damage, block travel, spike supply costs. Physical consequences, no ambiguity about what's happening.
- **Damage type:** Health, Supplies, Mobility

### 👽 ALIENS / DISCLOSURE — The Ambiguity Weapon

*The government confirms nothing. That's somehow worse than confirmation.*

- **Months:** March (leaked footage), July (congressional hearing), October (October Surprise)
- **Mechanic:** Ambiguity IS the damage type. UAP events drain Hope and Morale via uncertainty, not health. Party members fracture along belief/disbelief lines. Perception cascade does more damage than any physical event.
- **Damage type:** Hope, Morale, Party Cohesion
- **Special:** Alien events can trigger "ontological shock" debuff — affected party member is temporarily useless while reconsidering their place in the cosmos.

### 🐙 CTHULHU / COSMIC HORROR — The Certain-of-Incomprehensible

*The Old Ones are rising. The planning commission hasn't approved the zoning yet.*

- **Months:** February (strange dreams), April (Innsmouth anomalies), June (coastal incidents), September (cult proliferation), December (stars align)
- **Mechanic:** Sanity — a new stat. Unlike Hope (which drains from despair), Sanity drains from exposure to truths humans weren't meant to know. Low Sanity causes wrong choices to look like correct ones. Unlike Climate (predictable) or Aliens (ambiguous), Cthulhu events are certain — you just can't comprehend them. The horror is knowing something is happening and being unable to describe it to anyone.
- **Damage type:** Sanity, Hope
- **Special:** Sanity below 20 inverts some UI elements. Text gets… wrong. Options reorder. The game gaslights you.

### Theme Intensity (Per-Run)

Each run randomly weights the three themes. Some runs are mostly climate with alien whispers. Others are full cosmic horror with a side of heat dome. The tagline:

**"Every run is a different apocalypse."**

Intensity affects: event frequency from each pool, which mini-games appear, which months trigger major theme events, and which ending you're most likely to get.

## The Party

Pick 4 party members. Each has a job that gives minor bonuses and theme-specific traits:

- **Gig Worker** — hustles extra supplies, fragile morale. Climate: resistant to supply chain events. Aliens: "seen weirder stuff on the apps." Cthulhu: fragile sanity, high denial.
- **Tech Bro** — starts with more money, randomly loses skills to AI. Aliens: immediately monetizes everything ("alien contact ETF"). Cthulhu: tries to disrupt the Old Ones.
- **Teacher** — high morale boost for others, paid in peanuts. Climate: teaches survival skills. Cthulhu: sanity-resistant (graded middle school essays).
- **Boomer** — resistant to misinformation, can't use technology. Aliens: "I knew it." Immune to ontological shock. Cthulhu: "this is just like the sixties."
- **Gen Z** — high stamina, low budget, communicates exclusively in slang. Aliens: recovers fastest from shock ("this is literally just a vibe shift"). Cthulhu: already nihilistic, sanity barely moves.
- **Healthcare Worker** — heals party members, burned out. Climate: heals heat/exposure damage. Cthulhu: sanity drains faster (has seen things in the ER).
- **Influencer** — can "manifest" supplies (unreliable), high drama. Aliens: claims contact, gets book deal (+money, -hope for party). Cthulhu: starts a cult (accidentally).
- **Conspiracy Theorist** — immune to alien ambiguity, fragile sanity. Sees patterns everywhere. Some patterns are real. The game doesn't tell you which.
- **Cultist** — high sanity resistance, periodically drains party morale to "share the truth." Cthulhu events buff this character. Nobody asked them to join.

## Core Stats

- **Health** (0-100, everyone has it)
- **Morale** (party-wide, affects event outcomes)
- **Money** (shared, evaporates fast)
- **Supplies** (food, medicine, fuel, phone battery)
- **Hope** (secret stat — when it hits 0, game over even if alive)
- **Sanity** (party-wide, Cthulhu theme only — starts at 100, effects invert below 20)

## Random Events

Events are drawn from theme-specific pools weighted by run intensity. Some cross-theme events can fire regardless.

### Climate Events

- "Heat wave. Roll for health damage. Boomer is resistant."
- "Flooding destroyed the supply route. Pay 3x for food or hunt."
- "Wildfire evacuation. Lose 2 days of travel. Supplies may burn."
- "Your city hit 115°F. AC is a bidding war. Lose Money or lose Health."
- "A hurricane is named after someone you went to high school with. Morale drops."
- "Drought. Water rationing. Health slowly drains."
- "The air quality index is a color you've never seen before."

### Alien Events

- "A declassified Pentagon video shows something you can't explain. Roll for existential crisis. Gen Z: 'this is literally just a bird.' Recovers fastest."
- "Your Influencer claims alien contact. Book deal immediately. +Money, -Hope."
- "NORAD scrambles jets. It's a balloon. Again. -Morale."
- "Someone on Reddit solved the UAP mystery using Google Earth and a 2018 drone manual. It's more convincing than the official report."
- "A whistleblower testifies that the government has 'non-human biologics.' Congress asks about catering instead."
- "The Pentagon releases a statement. It is 14 pages. It says nothing. -Hope."
- "Your Tech Bro starts an alien disclosure blockchain. It briefly has market cap. It is now worthless."
- "A UFO is spotted over your city. Turns out to be Starlink. Gen Z knew the whole time."
- "NASA announces an independent UAP study. The conclusion is 'we need more data.' -Morale for everyone except Conspiracy Theorist, who feels vindicated."

### Cthulhu Events

- "A party member reads from the Necronomicon. It was on Kindle Unlimited."
- "The geometry of your apartment is wrong. Lose 1 day trying to find the bathroom."
- "R'lyeh rises. Insurance calls it an 'act of god(s).' Claim denied."
- "A deep one offers you a business opportunity. It's an MLM."
- "Your dreams show you the true face of the cosmos. It looks like a DMV waiting room."
- "Cthulhu stirs. The stock market doesn't notice."
- "Nyarlathotep appears as a management consultant. Offers to optimize your party. The optimization is your dissolution."
- "A shoggoth takes a job in sanitation. It's the most competent hire the city made all year."
- "You find a copy of the King in Yellow. Act I is a LinkedIn post."
- "The Cultist claims the stars are speaking to them. The stars are sending spam."
- "A non-Euclidean angle appears in your living room. The landlord says it's a feature."
- "Azathoth's court musicians warm up. It sounds like a corporate team-building exercise."

### Cross-Theme Events (can fire regardless of intensity)

- "Climate + Aliens: A UFO was actually a weather balloon full of microplastics."
- "Aliens + Cthulhu: Whistleblower testimony suggests 'non-human biologics' may include something from the deep. Everyone is uncomfortable."
- "Climate + Cthulhu: The hurricane is named Cthulhu. NOAA insists this is a coincidence."
- "All three: A congressional hearing covers UAPs, coastal anomalies, AND climate data. Nobody asks a useful question about any of them."

## Mini-Games

Theme-specific mini-games replace the base mini-game when that theme's intensity is dominant for the current month.

### Base Mini-Games (no dominant theme)

- **Hunting** → "Gig Hunting" — scroll through fake job postings, dodge scams
- **River Crossing** → "Fording Bureaucracy" — renew a license at the DMV
- **Fishing** → "Side Hustle Fishing" — catch freelance gigs, some are scams
- **Trading** → "Marketplace" — trade with other survivors, get scammed sometimes

### 👽 Alien Mini-Game: "Congressional Briefing"

*Replaces Fording Bureaucracy when Alien intensity is dominant.*

Extract actionable information from a UAP hearing. Multiple choice, every answer is technically true and completely useless:
- **Ask a follow-up question** → briefing ends, escorted out. Gain nothing.
- **Accept the answer** → lose Hope. Continue playing.
- **Leak the transcript** → gain Morale, risk "national security" debuff (lose random supplies).
- **Start a podcast about it** → Influencer gains Money, everyone else loses Hope.
- **Demand crash retrieval materials** → Conspiracy Theorist buffed. Everyone else: ontological shock check.

### 🐙 Cthulhu Mini-Game: "The Ritual"

*Replaces Hunting when Cthulhu intensity is dominant.*

Perform an eldritch ritual from a poorly translated text:
- **Wrong incantation** → lose Sanity, nothing happens
- **Right incantation** → lose Sanity, gain supplies from… somewhere
- **Refuse to participate** → lose Morale (Cultist angry), but Sanity preserved
- **Film it for content** → Influencer gains followers. Sanity loss doubles.
- **Translate it yourself** → Conspiracy Theorist option. Small Sanity loss, large insight gain. Unlocks a secret.

### 🌍 Climate Mini-Game: "Disaster Prep"

*Replaces Trading when Climate intensity is dominant.*

Stockpile supplies before the next disaster:
- **Buy in bulk** → spend Money, gain Supplies. Efficient.
- **Panic buy** → spend more Money, neighbors hate you (-Morale), same Supplies.
- **Go feral** → Gig Worker option. Forage successfully. Random: could find supplies or poison ivy.
- **Rely on FEMA** → lose 3 turns waiting. Nothing arrives.

## Death Causes

### Climate Deaths
- "succumbed to heat exhaustion (AC was a luxury)"
- "succumbed to microplastics"
- "drowned in a 500-year flood (third one this decade)"
- "breathed the air"
- "was lost in the wildfire evacuation zone"
- "died of a climate-related disease that used to be tropical"

### Alien Deaths
- "died of ontological shock after a Pentagon press conference"
- "was taken. (Nobody noticed for three days.)"
- "was chemically dissected by something that left a note saying 'we come in peace'"
- "succumbed to the pod person. (The pod person was more productive.)"
- "was disappeared by a three-letter agency. Officially, they were 'never here.'"
- "touched the crashed object. The crash object touched back."
- "was replaced. The replacement files better TPS reports."

### Cthulhu Deaths
- "looked upon the face of the Old One and went mad. (Meds were out of network.)"
- "was consumed by Nyarlathotep. Nyarlathotep left a Yelp review."

- "Answer the Call of Cthulhu on hold for 45 minutes. Died waiting."
- "was unmade. Not killed — unmade. Retconned from the save file."
- "joined the Cult. It was a pyramid scheme. Still lost everything."

### General / Classic Deaths
- "died of despair"
- "was lost to the algorithm"
- "was outbid on a studio apartment"
- "perished in the doomscroll"
- "died of a totally preventable disease they read a blog post about"
- "was replaced by a chatbot"
- "died doing their own research"
- "was crushed by student debt (literally)"
- "lost the will to continue after reading the news"

## Endings

Multiple endings based on surviving party members, Hope, Sanity, and which theme dominated:

- **"You Made It"** — at least one survivor, Hope > 0. It's 2027. Congratulations. The bar is on the floor.
- **"The Good Timeline"** — 3+ survivors, Hope > 50, Sanity > 50. You're not okay, but you're okay-er than most.
- **"Climate Victory"** — survived a climate-dominant run. The planet is still dying but YOU lived. Hero? No. Lucky? Yes.
- **"Disclosure"** — survived an alien-dominant run. You know the truth. So does everyone else. Nobody cares.
- **"The Dreamer"** — survived a Cthulhu-dominant run. You're not sure you survived. The stars are still singing. You're still listening.
- **"Total Party Wipe"** — everyone dead. Standard Oregon Trail experience.
- **"Ascension"** — Sanity hit 0, but Hope survived. You didn't die. You became something else. The save file is corrupted.

## Tombstone

Classic Oregon Trail tombstone. Players can write an epitaph. Future runs can see them. Tombstones persist across runs and may be from dead party members from previous playthroughs.

Cthulhu-dominant runs: some tombstones appear in languages that don't exist. Some appear before the character dies.

## Visual Style

- Pixel art aesthetic or simple CSS/canvas

- **Cthulhu theme:** When Sanity < 20, UI elements degrade. Text shifts subtly. Menu options reorder. The game itself becomes unreliable.
- **Alien theme:** Brief "redacted" flickers. Black bar censorship on random text elements during disclosure months.
